Somos., An Exhibition


In recent years, the growth of digital platforms and streaming content have changed not only the way of viewing but also making audiovisual productions. This has also modified the way of telling stories, the interaction with different audiences and the way in which these audiences approach and understand both fiction and non-fiction, including historical facts. Without replacing books, digital platforms have become a way to complement how people learn about contemporary history, nurturing narratives by telling and exposing social realities and complex situations at the civic and political levels.


From this premise came the idea of producing and making Somos., a fiction series based on the shocking report "Anatomy of a Massacre", which was written by renowned journalist Ginger Thompson about an event that took place in Allende, Coahuila in 2011.


Somos. was created and produced by James Schamus, producer of films such as Brokeback Mountain and Crounching Tiger, Hidden Dragon, . He wrote the series along with Mexican nationals Monika Revilla and Fernanda Melchor, and it is the result of a multidisciplinary and creative process. The plot centers around a true story but does so through fiction. In it, a horrible tragedy is told through the voices of its victims, but not in a detached or "impartial" way as it might be done in journalism. Rather, it is humanized by illustrating the habits and customs of a population whose daily life is shown through the complexity of living in a territory in which a hybrid culture is disrupted by the dynamics of the border area between Mexico and the United States.


This digital exhibition was created to accompany the launch of this project and seeks to show the principles and creation processes of the series. Three thematic units reveal the process of working as a team, their way of interpreting and considering the facts to then transform them into fiction and create characters capable of telling this terrifying story from a decidedly human perspective.


We decided to embark on the challenge of putting on this exhibition knowing that the Covid-19 pandemic would cause it to be presented remotely. Therefore it is an exclusively digital exhibition that takes place within the platform of the Museum of Memory and Tolerance. The virtual experience that we chose does not simulate cultural resources or physical elements, but instead uses language and tools typical of a digital medium. Foremost, in the three sections visitors will find the voices of the creators and promoters of the series with regard to the creative process. In the second unit, photographs coming from the eyes of the production team show how this heartbreaking story was transformed into spaces, environments and images to construct a fictional universe always parallel to real events. The last unit highlights the rich and diverse culture of northeastern Mexico, which is an essential part of the creation of the series and the world of Somos. With short clips and still images, we facilitate a journey through eight themes from the series, bringing the characters to life and fitting them into the story with consistency, while zooming in on the culture of a unique place like Coahuila, just a few miles from the border of Mexico and the United States.


We trust that this exhibition will enrich imaginations, broaden the audiovisual experience and help viewers to understand the creative process involved in these new formats that enable us to enhance culture while generating critical awareness.
