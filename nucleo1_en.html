Behind Somos. 


Somos., is the first Netflix series produced by James Schamus entirely in Spanish. It is also the screenwriting debut of the multi-award-winning Mexican writer Fernanda Melchor, and it additionally benefits from the experienced writing and vision of Monika Revilla, who has stood out for being involved in social projects that bring forward issues associated with the LGBTI + community.


For the first part of the exhibition’s digital experience, we tasked ourselves with bringing together the voices of these three creatives, who are the heart and soul of Somos. We want the viewer to get a closer look at the different moments and processes in the creation of a fiction series that is based on a dramatic real-life event. Through very specific interviews, we learn about their keen points of view and their different ways of describing a tragic event, as such giving a voice to the victims and re-creating their worlds and personal stories. Schamus, Melchor and Revilla emphasize the importance of having Ginger Thompson's article as a starting point and delve into their personal reasons for telling this story. They present anecdotes associated with the making of the series and highlight the importance of teamwork, a multicultural vision, and the idea of fiction being another way of accessing reality. Finally, they reiterate with genuine conviction their intention of affording visibility to a community that to date has not received justice and seems to have been forgotten.


This section allows us to understand the process of a technically complex project with delicate emotions. The result is a deeply human piece that, based on a very specific event, becomes fiction on the screen, and portrays the tragedy of a country plagued by violence and drug trafficking.
